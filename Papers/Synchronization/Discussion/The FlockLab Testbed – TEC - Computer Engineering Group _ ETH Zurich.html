The FlockLab Testbed

Main con­tent

Flock­Lab is a sec­ond gen­er­a­tion wire­less sen­sor net­work test­bed. It fea­tures a co-lo­cated and tightly cou­pled ob­server plat­form di­rectly con­nected to every WSN node un­der test (tar­get de­vice). The ob­servers can re­pro­gram tar­get de­vices, log data se­r­ial ports, trace and stim­u­late dig­i­tal GPIOs, con­trol the power sup­ply and pro­file power con­sump­tion. Us­ing an ex­ter­nal trac­ing unit, Flock­Lab is ca­pa­ble of trac­ing at very high rates (100s kbit/sec) and with high net­work syn­chro­niza­tion. This al­lows to cre­ate test­ing sce­nar­ios that are much more re­al­is­tic and hence com­plex than with stan­dard bus-based test­bed ar­chi­tec­tures. The test­bed is set up on cam­pus in and around our de­part­ment build­ing with re­cent ex­ten­sions ac­cross the city Zurich al­low­ing for joint in- and out­door test­ing of WSN ap­pli­ca­tions.

If you use Flock­Lab for your re­search be sure to prop­erly cite the test­bed in your work:

Ro­man Lim, Fed­erico Fer­rari, Marco Zim­mer­ling, Christoph Walser, Philipp Som­mer, and Jan Beu­tel. Flock­Lab: A Test­bed for Dis­trib­uted, Syn­chro­nized Trac­ing and Pro­fil­ing of Wire­less Em­bed­ded Sys­tems. In Pro­ceed­ings of the 12th In­ter­na­tional Con­fer­ence on In­for­ma­tion Pro­cess­ing in Sen­sor Net­works (IPSN '13), ACM, New York, NY, USA, 153-166, 2013, https://​doi.​org/​10.​1145/​2461381.​2461402.
